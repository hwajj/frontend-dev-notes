## 5/4 월

**A:** I need to vent for a second. Work has been really frustrating lately.
**B:** Uh oh, what happened this time?

**A:** So we got the planning doc and the design draft, right?
**A:** But the planning was kind of all over the place, so the designers just did their own thing.

**B:** Let me guess… now nothing matches?

**A:** Exactly. There are features in the design that weren't even in the plan.
**A:** So now I have to go back and ask both sides what's going on.

**B:** And they didn't even agree with each other beforehand?

**A:** Nope. The planner did their version, the designer did theirs.
**A:** And now neither of them is giving me a straight answer.

**B:** That's the worst. You're stuck in the middle.

**A:** Seriously. I'm like, "What am I even supposed to build here?"

**B:** Do planners usually just go along with whatever the designers add?

**A:** Sometimes, yeah. Unless it's something they feel strongly about.
**A:** Otherwise, they tend to just let it slide.

**B:** But then the logic behind the feature isn't fully thought out, right?

**A:** Exactly. That's the whole problem.
**A:** So I end up asking a ton of questions just to sync everything up.

**B:** Sounds like extra work for you.

**A:** It is. And the worst part?
**A:** When I ask, it takes like one or two days to even get a response.

**B:** You're kidding. And they still expect you to meet the deadline?

**A:** Of course. The deadline is non-negotiable.

**B:** So if something's unclear and you don't ask, it's your fault…
**B:** But if you do ask, you're stuck waiting?

**A:** Exactly. You just can't win.

**B:** That's rough.

**A:** So basically, developers are just expected to fill in the gaps themselves.
**A:** If anything's missing, we're supposed to catch it and fix it.

**B:** That's a lot to put on one person.

**A:** Yeah. And if something goes wrong later, they'll say,
**A:** "Why didn't you flag this earlier?"

**B:** Classic blame game.

**A:** Every time.

**B:** Honestly, it sounds like a process issue more than anything.

**A:** Yeah… but it's hard to change that kind of culture.

**B:** Especially when leadership thinks everything's running fine.

**A:** Right. Once people at the top are convinced things are working,
**A:** they stop listening to feedback from below.

**B:** Unless it comes from someone with authority.

**A:** Or an external consultant they paid a lot of money for.

**B:** Yep. Then suddenly everyone's all ears.

## 자주 쓰는 표현 정리 (영한)

1. **I need to vent**: 하소연 좀 할게, 속 얘기 좀 해야겠어
   - I need to vent — do you have a minute? / 나 좀 털어놔야 할 것 같아, 잠깐 시간 돼?
   - She just needed to vent, not get advice. / 걔는 그냥 하소연이 하고 싶었던 거지, 조언을 원한 게 아니야.

2. **all over the place**: 두루뭉술한, 뒤죽박죽인
   - The report was all over the place. / 보고서가 뒤죽박죽이었어.
   - My thoughts are all over the place today. / 오늘 생각이 너무 산만해.

3. **do their own thing**: 자기 방식대로 하다, 멋대로 하다
   - The designers just did their own thing. / 디자이너들이 그냥 자기들 멋대로 했어.
   - He doesn't follow rules — he just does his own thing. / 그는 규칙을 안 따라. 그냥 자기 방식대로 해.

4. **let me guess**: 맞혀볼게, 어디 보자 (예상을 먼저 말할 때)
   - Let me guess — it was his idea? / 맞혀볼게, 걔 아이디어였지?
   - Let me guess… you forgot again. / 어디 보자… 또 잊어버린 거지.

5. **stuck in the middle**: 중간에 끼이다
   - I'm always stuck in the middle when they argue. / 걔들 싸울 때마다 나만 중간에 끼여.
   - Developers are stuck in the middle between planners and designers. / 개발자는 기획과 디자인 사이에 끼인 존재야.

6. **what am I supposed to ~?**: 나보고 어쩌라는 거야, ~을 어떻게 해야 하는 거지?
   - What am I supposed to build here? / 나보고 뭘 만들라는 거야?
   - What am I supposed to say to that? / 그 말에 나보고 뭐라고 하라는 거야?

7. **feel strongly about ~**: ~에 대해 소신이 있다, 강하게 생각하다
   - Unless it's something they feel strongly about, they just let it go. / 소신 있는 부분이 아니면 그냥 넘겨.
   - I feel strongly about this issue. / 이 문제에 대해선 소신이 있어.

8. **let it slide**: 그냥 넘어가다, 눈감아주다
   - I'll let it slide this time. / 이번엔 그냥 넘어갈게.
   - They tend to let things slide when the deadline is close. / 데드라인이 가까우면 그냥 넘어가는 편이야.

9. **sync (everything) up**: 서로 맞추다, 싱크를 맞추다
   - We need to sync up before the meeting. / 회의 전에 서로 맞춰봐야 해.
   - I ask questions just to sync everything up. / 서로 맞추려고 질문을 하게 되는 거야.

10. **you're kidding**: 말도 안 돼, 농담이지?
    - You're kidding — they still expect you to finish on time? / 말도 안 돼, 그래도 제때 끝내길 바라는 거야?
    - You're kidding me right now. / 지금 농담하는 거지.

11. **non-negotiable**: 절대 바꿀 수 없는, 협상 불가
    - The deadline is non-negotiable. / 데드라인은 절대 못 바꿔.
    - Sleep is non-negotiable for me. / 나한테 수면은 타협 불가야.

12. **you can't win**: 어떻게 해도 답이 없다, 진퇴양난
    - If you speak up, you're difficult. If you don't, you're passive. You can't win. / 말하면 까다롭다고 하고, 안 하면 수동적이라고 해. 답이 없어.
    - With some people, you just can't win. / 어떤 사람들과는 그냥 답이 없어.

13. **fill in the gaps**: 빈 부분을 채우다, 부족한 부분을 알아서 메우다
    - Developers are expected to fill in the gaps. / 개발자가 빠진 부분을 알아서 채워야 해.
    - Can you fill in the gaps for me? / 빠진 부분 좀 설명해줄 수 있어?

14. **blame game**: 책임 떠넘기기
    - I'm tired of the blame game. / 책임 떠넘기기에 지쳤어.
    - When things go wrong, the blame game always starts. / 뭔가 잘못되면 항상 책임 떠넘기기가 시작돼.

15. **all ears**: 귀 기울이고 있다, 듣고 있다
    - Pay a consultant enough, and suddenly everyone's all ears. / 컨설턴트한테 돈만 많이 주면 갑자기 다들 귀가 열려.
    - Go ahead, I'm all ears. / 말해봐, 듣고 있어.
